Project Idea

Overview:

I intend on developing a smartphone app titled i-Job that will feature a platform to connect job seekers directly with the individual advertising a job/task. The app will feature a location-based interface design that sets pins in the exact places a job is being advertised. The individual advertising the job/task will prefer to use this app due to its easy-to-use design as well as the fact that they can sit back and wait for a job offer. A wide range of job types can be filtered by users from tradesmen through to delivery drivers. I aim to develop this app for both iOS and android devices using Swift, Objective C, Java, C++, etc. Its important to note that there are two app variations the employer app and the worker app. 

i-Job

Motivation:

i-Job ensures that people struggling to find work can always earn money by requesting and then completing available jobs/tasks near them. It also ensures people wanting a job to be completed don’t need to scroll through the web to find someone viable. The app targets both unemployed individuals seeking work and people having trouble finding someone to do their job. The current unemployment rate in Australia is 4.2% which is a relatively big market of people seeking work. i-Job is a free to use and easily accessible software solution. 

Description:

In order for the app to work effectively there will need to be two interfaces - the employer app and the worker app. 
   
          Employer App 
An employer app is necessary for employers to advertise their job, receive job notifications, message workers directly, and track whether the worker is at the job location. 

The employer app must have the following features:

-           REGISTRATION AND PROFILE MANAGEMENT: By providing a drivers licence as well as other information we can ensure the employer is above the  age of 18. Also, this feature ensures the employer’s personal information is safety stored in the case of a work-related incident. 
-           GEOLOCATION: Direct access to Google Maps through the app to get a bird's-eye view of where the employee is and to see if he/she is at the job site.
-           MESSAGING: To communicate with the worker and settle where the job is to be complete.
-           REQUESTING INTERFACE: As multiple people may request a certain job the employer can select the best worker based on their user rating and previous experience. 
-           RATINGS & REVIEWS: Providing feedback and rate the worker. Also, there may be an option to view the workers employer history and past jobs. -           PAYMENT METHOD INTERGRATION: To pay the worker when the job/task is complete. 

          Worker App 
A worker app for workers is an important platform through which workers can view all of the jobs/tasks near them and request to complete the job with no hassles. 

The worker app must have:

-           REGISTRATION AND PROFILE MANAGEMENT: Like the employer app this ensures both parties are protected in the case of an incident. Furthermore, the feature of an active profile which displays the workers rating and history is essential for the employer to see. 
-           GEOLOCATION: Direct access to Google Maps through the app to get a bird’s-eye view of the jobs/tasks being advertised and to see whether they are nearby. 
-           MESSAGING: To communicate with the employer directly and ask any questions regarding job requirements. 
-           REQUESTING INTERFACE: The worker can request a job that suits them. 
-           LIVE JOB TRACKING: Real time tracking of job offers. 
-           PAYMENT METHOD INTERGRATION: To ensure the worker gets paid at the end of a job. 

As part of the development process, mobile user interface design is also necessary for the construction of the i-Job app. The i-Job app should offer a user-friendly interface and be responsive. Customers expect a consistent experience in which they know exactly what to do with the app, which buttons to press, and so on. Also, relating to the user interface i-Job will need to be aesthetically pleasing and look professional – this can be achieved through eye-grabbing colour schemes, high resolution photos, etc. Because flexibility is key in mobile app development i-Job will be available on both iOS and Android, and as such will need to be edited for many different screen sizes and resolutions. Internet security is an ever-increasing issue - because i-Job will be storing personal and sensitive information (e.g., credit and debit card details) the app will need to be very secure. Furthermore, it is important that I include a feedback system for users to report bugs, suggest new features, or provide criticism. To make the initial experience of using the app easy I will provide “how-to” screens to provide information on how to use different features. 

Tools and Technologies:

Computer hardware is any physical device used in or with your machine, whereas software is a collection of code installed onto your computer's hard drive. In the prototyping stage of my i-Job app development I will be using Adobe XD to create screen designs and interactions for an enhanced user experience. To develop i-Job as an iOS app I will need a Mac computer running the latest version of Xcode. Xcode is Apple’s Integrated Development Environment for both Mac and iOS apps. This step will also require me to thoroughly learn Swift and Objective C. Also, I will need to learn Java and C++ in order to code the Android app. The minimum hardware requirements for Android Studio are listed below:

-           A 64-bit environment is required for Android 2.3. and higher versions.
-           At least 250GB of free disk space to check out the code and an extra 150 GB to build it. 
-           Java Development Kit (JDK).

The skills required to develop my i-Job mobile app are listed below:

-           The ability to work on multiple programming languages 

-           Knowledge of Back-end technologies 

-           Cross-platform app development 

-           UI/UX design 

-           Knowledge of cybersecurity 

-           Problem solving skills 

-           Analytical thinking 

-           Creativity 

-           Product management skills 

-           Agile development 

After the app has been created, I will also need good business skills and to be able to work with teams as well as clients. Because the app contains many features and is fairly complex it may cost over $30’000 to produce. I will need to enter into a few courses to gain the necessary programming skills which may also cost a substantial amount of money. However, the hardware and software required to create the project shouldn’t cost that much. There may also be times throughout the production process when I need to outsource other app developers. 

Outcome: 
If i-Job is created the app will help a lot of people find work in these difficult times and will provide an effective marketing platform for people advertising jobs/tasks. The app will help to increase employment rates particularly in metropolitan areas. It’s easy to use design enables uses to quickly pick up some extra money serving someone else. Despite all of the position impacts the app has there may be unseen consequences from people relying on the app for employment - as apps can often crash and be hacked.
